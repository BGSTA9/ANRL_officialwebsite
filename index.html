Our Story

Why We Exist

Argo Navis is an independent research laboratory dedicated solely to Number Theory. We conduct research through computational number theory by building high-fidelity simulations and training deep neural networks to uncover the hidden algorithmic patterns governing the nature of reality.

We investigate the deep structure of integers, specifically prime number properties ‚Äî targeting persistent open problems in number theory using simulation where analytical proofs have stalled. Our findings extend to cryptography, coding theory, and other critical real-world systems.

We view integers not as a logical structure alone, but as a vast, high-dimensional dataset. We don't just verify known results ‚Äî we build "digital laboratories" that stress-test conjectures and discover hidden algorithms before formal proofs are attempted.

01

Compute Over Conjecture

We simulate before we prove. High-fidelity data guides intuition. If simulation doesn't support the hypothesis, we discard the hypothesis, not the data.

02

Output Over Credentials

We value what you can build, not where you studied. A working GitHub repository holds more weight than a transcript. Ideas are judged by predictive power and mathematical validity.

03

Radical Transparency

We build in public. Datasets, simulation engines, and pre-print drafts are open-source by default. The future of science is decentralized and collaborative.

04

The "Deciphering" Spirit

Reality is a puzzle, not a black box. We are obsessively curious to find signal in noise. We interrogate AI models to understand the geometric logic they've learned.

What We Look For

ANReLa is built on the principle that great research comes from diverse minds united by obsessive curiosity. We value demonstrable skill, mathematical rigor, and the drive to build systems that advance our understanding of prime structures.

  • Strong computational skills (Python, Julia, or equivalent)
  • Experience with deep learning frameworks
  • Interest in number theory and algebraic structures
  • Working GitHub portfolio or equivalent open-source contributions
  • Self-directed research capability
  • Comfort with open science and public collaboration

Don't meet every criterion? If you're driven by genuine curiosity about the arithmetic structure of reality, we'd still love to hear from you.
